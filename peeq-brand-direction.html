peeq
↑ mirror axis

The name contains the product. p and q are mirror glyphs — a reflection, which is exactly what the app is. The double ee in the middle reads as two eyes taking a peek. Nothing needs to be invented; the wordmark just needs to make this visible.

logo

Wordmark first, eyes second

Primary mark: lowercase peeq, custom-drawn geometric monoline. Brief for the logotype: the p and q must be exact horizontal mirrors of each other (same bowl, same stem, flipped), and the two e's should sit slightly closer together than normal tracking so they pair up as a face. Always lowercase, everywhere — packaging, legal pages, app store. Never "Peeq" or "PEEQ".

Secondary mark / app icon: just the ee. It's the shortest possible version of the brand and it blinks.

App icon concepts — violet is the default store listing. The blink is the brand's one signature animation (see motion).

Clearspace rule: keep space equal to the height of one "e" around the wordmark on all sides. Minimum size: 72px wide on screen before switching to the ee icon.
color

A warm stage, one loud guest

The app's real content is people's own photos. UI color has to flatter skin tones and never compete with garments — so the base palette is warm and quiet, and exactly one color is allowed to be loud.

ink#1A1714
All text, primary buttons. A warm black — never pure #000.
fitting-room white#FAF6F0
Default background. Warm whites flatter skin in photos.
peeq violet#6D5BFF
Interactive only: links, scan moments, active states. Never for body text under 18px (contrast ≈ 4.3:1 on white).
butter#F5DD90
Highlights, badges, "new" stickers, empty states. Trending in fashion right now; use in small doses.
stone#8A8378
Secondary text, captions, prices.
Retiring: the current EasyFitCheck green #2A3D2F goes away entirely. Don't blend the two identities during migration — cut over in one release.
typography

One system, two scripts

The non-negotiable constraint: everything must look equally good in Devanagari and Latin, because the product ships bilingual. Both families below carry native Devanagari that was designed together with the Latin — no mismatched fallback fonts.

ui direction

Minimal means the clothes win

1
The photo is the interface.

The try-on result owns the screen edge-to-edge. Controls float over it or sit in a bottom sheet — never a persistent chrome frame around the person.

2
One accent, one job.

Violet appears only where a tap does something. If a screen has violet in three places, the screen has too many actions.

3
Round everything that's touchable.

Pill buttons, 20px card radii, soft shadows. Matches the roundness of Baloo and makes the whole app feel like the wordmark.

4
Thumb-first, always.

Primary actions live in the bottom third. The core flow (scan → photo → peeq → send lead) must be completable one-handed on a budget Android phone.

5
Both languages are first-class.

Language toggle visible on every screen, never buried in settings. Layouts must survive Nepali strings running ~20% longer.

motion & photography

A blink and a peek — that's it

Two motions define the brand, used sparingly:

a
The blink.

The ee icon blinks once (scaleY 1 → 0.08 → 1, ~140ms) on app open and while a try-on renders. It replaces generic spinners — the app is "looking".

b
The peek.

New content enters by peeking up from the bottom edge (translateY 12px + fade, 240ms, ease-out). Cards, sheets, toasts — everything arrives the same way.

Photography: mirror-selfie framing, phone flash, real bodies of all sizes — never studio models on seamless backgrounds. Marketing imagery should look like something a shopper actually made in the app two minutes ago. Respect prefers-reduced-motion across the board.

guardrails

Do / don't

do

  • keep the wordmark lowercase in every context
  • let user photos fill the screen; UI floats above
  • use violet only for tappable things
  • write "peeq it" as the universal try-on verb
  • design every screen in Nepali first, then English
  • use butter for playful moments (badges, empty states)

don't

  • no gradients on buttons or text
  • no pure black (#000) or cool grays
  • no drop shadows heavier than 10% ink
  • no all-caps anywhere except legal fine print
  • no spinners — the blink is the loading state
  • no mixing old EasyFitCheck green into new screens
