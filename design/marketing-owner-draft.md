# peeq — store-owner campaign · copy draft v1

Voice rules: lowercase where the brand speaks. Short. A little cheeky, never salesy-slick.
"peeq" is a verb. The pain we sell against is always the same DM: **"will this suit me?"**
Primary CTA everywhere → `peeq.ai/owner` (or /owner on the current domain). Nepali lines are
not translations — same energy, rewritten.

---

## 1 · one-liners (bios, headers, stickers)

- send once. they see it on them.
- your rack, on every shopper.
- the trial room, in their pocket.
- stop answering "will this suit me?" — show them.
- a little look before they buy. a lot fewer returns.
- ne: किन्नु अघि उनीहरूकै जीउमा देखाउनुहोस्।
- ne: "मलाई सुहाउँछ?" — अब जवाफ होइन, फोटो पठाउनुहोस्।

## 2 · instagram ads / boosted posts

**A — the DM pain (sellers who sell via DM)**
> every day, the same DM: "will this suit me?" 😩
> now you reply with a link. they take one photo. they see the outfit on *their own body*.
> "wow it looks perfect 😍" — that's the next message you get.
> peeq — virtual try-on for instagram stores. free to start → link in bio

**B — the numbers angle (established shops)**
> your rack already knows what will sell this month.
> peeq tells you: most-tried pieces, try-ons per day, and every "I want this" with a
> name, size and phone number.
> put a try-on QR on every hanger. live in one afternoon. → peeq.ai/owner

**C — wedding season push (time 6–8 weeks before mangsir / baishakh–jestha)**
> she has 40 saris to consider and one afternoon in new road.
> let her see every single one on herself — before she reaches your shop.
> peeq: the whole rack, tried on from home. book your setup before the season → /owner

## 3 · reel script (~20s, founder or shop-owner voiceover)

1. (2s) close-up: phone buzzing — DM: "will this suit me??" ×3
2. (3s) owner sighs, types… stops. sends a link instead.
3. (5s) shopper POV: taps link → one selfie → outfit appears ON HER. reaction shot.
4. (4s) DM comes back: "wow 😍 M size, keeping it!" — lead lands in dashboard with name+size.
5. (3s) owner smiles, flips garment to "reserved".
6. (3s) end card: **send once. they see it on them.** — peeq · free to start · /owner
   (ee blinks once on the end card — the brand's one signature animation)

## 4 · WhatsApp / DM cold outreach (shop owners)

> namaste [name] dai/didi 🙏 [shop] ko collection instagram ma dekhyo — dami cha!
> hami peeq banaudai chhau: customer le afnai photo bata tapaiko kapda afu-mai lagayera
> herna milcha (virtual try-on). "malai suhauncha?" bhanne DM ko jawaf — ek link.
> tapaiko pasal ko lagi 2 hafta free trial garna man lagcha? setup hami garidinchhau,
> ek din pani laagdaina. demo: [link]

Follow-up (3 days, if seen no reply):
> ek 30-second demo video pathauchu hai — afnai ek piece tapai mathi try garera dekhauchu 😄
> photo matra pathaidinus garment ko.

## 5 · in-person pitch (new road / durbarmarg walk-in, 60 seconds)

Open the kiosk on a tablet, photograph *their* sari off the rack, try it on the shopkeeper
or yourself, on the spot. The demo is the pitch. Then:

> "every customer who scans this hanger tag sees this sari on themselves. the ones who
> love it send you their name and size. you'll also see which 5 pieces got tried the most
> this week — that's what you restock. two weeks free, we photograph the rack for you."

Leave-behind card copy (front): **send once. they see it on them.** (back): QR → /owner,
"free 2-week pilot · we photograph your rack · works on the phone you already have"

## 6 · objections → answers (for whoever is selling)

- "customers won't bother" → they already ask "will this suit me?" — this *is* the answer, one tap.
- "photos of my customers?" → never stored; the photo lives on their phone, deleted after.
- "too technical for us" → we set it up in one afternoon and photograph the rack ourselves.
- "how much?" → free to start; paid plans only once try-ons prove themselves. (pilot: free 2 weeks.)
- "AI looks fake" → show, don't argue: try their bestseller on them right there.
